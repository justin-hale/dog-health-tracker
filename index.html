Catch him sleeping and count his breaths for 30 seconds × 2. Under 30 = normal. If 40+ call Justin. Over 40+ = ER immediately.

Give Pimobendan, Enalapril, and Furosemide together, hidden in a small piece of bread or treat. Do not feed his meal yet — Pimobendan requires an empty stomach and 1 hour before food.

Scoop 1/2 cup of pre-soaked kibble from the jar. Add 2 spoons of Kirkland wet food (pink can) on top. Fresh water must always be available.

~9–10 PM · One hour after medications

How to Check Breathing Rate

Do this once per day whenever you catch him sleeping. The single most important monitoring task.

  1. 1 Wait until Gizmo is fully asleep or resting quietly (not panting, not moving).
  2. 2 Watch his chest or belly rise and fall. Each rise-and-fall = one breath.
  3. 3 Count breaths for exactly 30 seconds.
  4. 4 Multiply by 2 to get breaths per minute.
  5. 5
    Under 30 = Normal
    30–40 = Call Justin/Christina
    Over 40 = Go to ER immediately

All three meds are given together before meals. Never skip. Never double up if you miss a dose.

Strengthens heart muscle. Must be given on an empty stomach — wait 1 full hour before feeding. Give hidden in a small piece of plain bread alongside the other meds.

Prep Method
  1. 1 Night before: 1 cup dry kibble in a mason jar, add plain water to cover. Refrigerate overnight. No broth or stock — high sodium is dangerous.
  2. 2 At mealtime, scoop 1/2 cup soaked kibble into his bowl.
  3. 3 Add 2 spoons Kirkland wet food (pink can) on top.
  4. 4 Serve at room temperature. Refrigerate the jar and open wet food cans.

What's Normal for Gizmo

Don't be alarmed by these — expected given his condition and medications.

Frequent or unusual urination

He takes a diuretic which empties his bladder. He may try to mark several dry spots before producing a stream. This is normal. Always keep fresh water available.

Resting more than a young dog

Normal for his age and condition. Good days = interested in food, alert, wants interaction. Bad days = very lethargic, not interested in food. Call Justin/Christina if you see a bad day.

Call Justin/Christina if you notice:

Resting breathing rate 30–35/min · Gums pale pink · Refusing food for more than one meal · Straining when urinating · Blood in urine · Noticeably less energy

Go to the ER Immediately If You See:
Labored or rapid breathing at rest

Count breaths × 2. Over 40 = call ER now.

Blue, gray, or white gums

Gums should be pink and moist. Any other color = call ER now.

Collapse or inability to stand

He has had collapse episodes before. Do not wait. Call ER now.

Coughing up foam or fluid

Indicates fluid in the lungs. Call ER now.

Activity & Handling
  • He can roam the yard on his own — he knows his limits. No leash needed for bathroom trips.
  • Keep calm and quiet — stress can trigger episodes. Low-key cuddle time is perfect.
  • No rough play — gentle interaction only. No wrestling or intense running.
  • Watch for overexertion — if he pants heavily after mild activity, let him rest immediately.
  • Comfortable temperature — avoid excessive heat or cold. Small dogs with heart conditions are more sensitive.
About Gizmo

Gizmo is a tough little guy who has been through a lot — he was recently hospitalized and is on a recovery path. Good signs: interested in food, wants attention, curious about his surroundings, comfortable breathing at rest. He loves his routine and does best with calm, consistent care.

If something seems off and you're not sure — call Justin/Christina first. It's never a bother. Gizmo is very much loved and his family trusts you with his care.
